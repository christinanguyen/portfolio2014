A definitive moment in my design journey was when my professor at VCUarts told me, “I would like to see the design sensibilities you display in your print to be translated in your web work.”

My humble roots began with learning to customize my AOL profile when I was 12, which subsequently led to me being a self-taught Photoshop wiz-kid that got overzealous with filters. I later on went to school for graphic design, where I learned invaluable design fundamentals by venturing out of my comfort zone: the world of print.

The foray into print has attributed to much of my development as a digital designer. I’ve grown a lot since then, my penchant towards careful yet artful restraint in designs becoming more evident in my body of work.
